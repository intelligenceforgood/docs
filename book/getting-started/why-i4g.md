# Why i4g

Every year, millions of people lose money and trust to online fraud —
romance scams, crypto schemes, impersonation, fake employment offers. Most
victims report to one agency, and that report sits in a silo. The scammer
moves on to the next victim using the same phone number, the same wallet
address, the same fake profile.

**i4g exists to connect the dots.**

## The problem

Fraud reporting is fragmented. A victim in the US reports a Bitcoin wallet
to the FTC. A victim in the UK reports the same wallet to Action Fraud.
Neither agency knows they're chasing the same scammer. The intelligence
never converges.

Meanwhile, scammers recycle infrastructure — the same email addresses,
crypto wallets, phone numbers, and domains appear across dozens of
independent reports. But without a system to aggregate, link, and analyze
those reports, each one looks like an isolated incident.

## What makes i4g different

i4g is a **threat intelligence aggregation platform** designed specifically
for fraud. It does four things that individual reporting systems cannot:

### 1. Extracts structured intelligence from messy reports

Scam victims write freeform narratives full of obfuscated contact details,
misspelled URLs, and fragmented evidence. i4g's extraction pipeline finds
the threat entities — wallet addresses, email addresses, phone numbers,
domains — even when scammers deliberately disguise them.

### 2. Links cases through shared threat entities

When two reports mention the same Bitcoin wallet or the same phone number,
i4g automatically connects them. Isolated reports become linked cases,
and linked cases reveal coordinated fraud campaigns.

### 3. Engages volunteer analysts at scale

University students, cybersecurity professionals, and trained volunteers
review and classify cases through a structured engagement model. This
powers a human-in-the-loop intelligence cycle that scales beyond what
any single agency can staff.

### 4. Produces actionable output for law enforcement

The platform generates signed dossiers, STIX threat intelligence bundles,
and structured data exports that law enforcement partners can ingest
directly — no re-keying, no lost context.

## Who uses i4g

| Role                        | What they do                                          |
| --------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| **Analysts**                | Review cases, classify fraud, investigate entities    |
| **Engagement participants** | Students in university programs reviewing cases       |
| **Law enforcement**         | Receive dossiers and intelligence feeds               |
| **Victims / reporters**     | Submit fraud reports through the intake form          |
| **Administrators**          | Manage users, engagements, and platform configuration |
