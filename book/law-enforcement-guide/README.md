# Law Enforcement Guide

Welcome, and thank you for your partnership. I4G is a nonprofit initiative
that aggregates victim fraud reports, extracts actionable intelligence, and
delivers evidence-rich products to support your investigations.

This guide explains how to work with I4G intelligence products — from
understanding report structure to verifying authenticity and requesting
additional evidence.

## What you'll receive

I4G analysts produce several types of intelligence products tailored for
law enforcement use:

| Product                  | Format          | What it contains                                           |
| ------------------------ | --------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| **PDF Dossier**          | PDF             | Case briefing with timeline, entity table, evidence        |
| **LEA Evidence Dossier** | PDF             | Formal evidence package with chain-of-custody manifests    |
| **STIX Bundle**          | JSON (STIX 2.1) | Machine-readable indicators for your threat intel tools    |
| **Evidence Package**     | ZIP archive     | Complete evidence files with per-artifact integrity hashes |

All products include digital signatures and tamper-evident audit trails.
See [Dossiers & Reports](../key-concepts/dossiers-and-reports.md) for
the full breakdown of report types.

## How to get started

1. Your I4G liaison will provide you with access credentials and an
   introduction to the reporting workflow.
2. Read [Working with Reports](working-with-reports.md) for the details
   on report structure, verification, and requesting additional evidence.
3. For questions about data handling or access to raw evidence, contact
   your liaison at `leo@intelligenceforgood.org`.

## Data handling expectations

- Treat all shared artifacts as **confidential** and for official use only.
- Do not forward reports to third parties without written consent from
  Intelligence for Good.
- Notify your liaison immediately if you detect compromised credentials or
  phishing attempts involving shared data.
- Reports default to **TLP:RED** (named recipients only) unless otherwise
  marked.
