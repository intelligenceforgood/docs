# Engagement Guide

Engagements are structured programs — university capstone projects, training
exercises, or operational rounds — where participants review fraud cases
under supervision. This section covers everything about working in and
managing engagements.

## What you'll learn

| Guide                                                | Who it's for           | What it covers                                |
| ---------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| [Working in an Engagement](working-in-engagement.md) | Analysts, students     | Selecting engagements, scoped views, workflow |
| [Managing Engagements](managing-engagements.md)      | Managers, coordinators | Creating, activating, monitoring, exporting   |
| [Leaderboard & Performance](leaderboard.md)          | Everyone               | Scoring formula, metrics, exports             |
| [Engagement Lifecycle](lifecycle.md)                 | Managers, admins       | Draft → Active → Completed → Archived stages  |

## New to engagements?

If you're an analyst joining a university engagement for the first time:

1. Read [Engagements](../key-concepts/engagements.md) in Key Concepts to
   understand what engagements are and why they exist.
2. Then follow [Working in an Engagement](working-in-engagement.md) for the
   hands-on walkthrough.

If you're a manager setting up your first engagement:

1. Start with [Managing Engagements](managing-engagements.md) for the
   step-by-step guide.
2. Review [Engagement Lifecycle](lifecycle.md) to understand how status
   transitions work.
3. Use [Leaderboard & Performance](leaderboard.md) to track your team's
   progress.
