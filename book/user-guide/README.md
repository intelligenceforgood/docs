# User Guide

If you've been affected by a scam, you're in the right place. I4G is a
nonprofit platform that helps victims report fraud and turns those reports
into actionable intelligence for law enforcement.

This guide walks you through submitting a report and following up on it.

## What happens when you report

1. **You submit a report** with details about the scam and any evidence you
   have (screenshots, chat logs, receipts).
2. **Our analysts review it** — trained volunteers classify the fraud type,
   extract key identifiers (wallet addresses, phone numbers, URLs), and
   assess the risk.
3. **Your report feeds intelligence** — the identifiers from your report are
   linked with other victims' reports, building a picture of the scam
   operation.
4. **Intelligence reaches law enforcement** — when patterns emerge, I4G
   produces evidence dossiers for law enforcement agencies.

Every report matters. Even if you lost nothing, your report may contain
identifiers that help connect other victims' cases.

## Your privacy

- Your contact information is **encrypted at rest** and not visible to
  analysts in its raw form.
- Analysts see redacted placeholders like `[VICTIM_EMAIL]` and
  `[VICTIM_PHONE]`.
- Decryption is limited to authorized roles and only done when necessary for
  the investigation.

To understand more about how information flows through the system, see
[How It Works](../getting-started/how-it-works.md).
